THE BATMAN - PART II: NEWS, CAST, STORY, & EVERYTHING WE KNOW

    The Batman was released in March 2022, ushering in a new era for The Dark Knight — here’s everything to know about The Batman - Part II, including updates on The Joker’s possible inclusion and the spin-off shows. Directed by Matt Reeves from a screenplay by him and Peter Craig, The Batman joins Robert Pattinson's DC hero in his second year as Gotham City’s vigilante. The ending of The Batman definitely left scope for a sequel, and the overwhelmingly positive reception to this latest iteration of the Caped Crusader made The Batman 2 feel almost guaranteed.

    While initially conceived to stand alone, the ending of The Batman set it up as the perfect movie to usher in a Batman-centered cinematic universe — including The Batman 2 as well as several possible spinoff shows. While The Batman 2 isn't on DC's immediate release slate, Matt Reeves is reportedly hard at work on a sequel. Here is everything to know about The Batman: Part II, including who in the cast might return, the sequel’s release date, and if the Barry Keoghan iteration of the Joker will finally get more screen time.

THE BATMAN II: LATEST NEWS & UPDATES

    Recent updates on The Batman - Part II from September 2024 interviews highlight its connections to the Penguin spinoff show. Director Matt Reeves described The Penguin as an "entrée into the next movie," set in winter, potentially with a Christmas backdrop for the sequel. Reeves and producer Dylan Clark hinted at Penguin's evolution, suggesting he will be a significantly changed character when he returns in the sequel..

    Additionally, screenwriter Mattson Tomlin revealed that filming is set to begin in 2025, aligning with the projected 2026 release date. Despite earlier concerns over a lack of updates, this news is promising for fans.

    There have been various rumors about the sequel, many of which DC co-CEO James Gunn has debunked. hile details on the central villain and supporting cast remain unclear, audiences shouldn't expect a trailer until late 2024. However, casting news and teasers are likely to emerge in the meantime to build anticipation.

THE BATMAN II IS CONFIRMED

    Following the commercial and critical success of The Batman, Warner Bros. announced a sequel and streaming series spinning out of The Batman — one (now-canceled) series about the Gotham City Police Department, a potential Arkham Asylum-based show, a series based on The Penguin, and possibly even one for Zoë Kravtiz' Catwoman. While the status of these ambitious TV projects is currently a little murky other than The Penguin, which is set to release in September 2024, The Batman - Part II very much isn't, as The Batman 2 has been confirmed.

    The recent shake-ups at the newly-merged Warner Bros Discovery, including the appointment of James Gunn and Peter Safran as heads of a new on-screen DC Universe, have put some future projects in doubt, but Gunn has confirmed that Reeves will be writing and directing two more The Batman movies, resulting in a trilogy. This news was met incredibly well by most — especially since it comes at a time when many popular DC screen-based ventures like the Arrowverse have concluded, leaving audiences more hungry than ever for new installments.

THE BATMAN II PRODUCTION STATUS

    In August 2024, screenwriter Mattson Tomlin announced that filming for The Batman - Part II is set to begin in 2025. Tomlin, who is co-writing the script with creator Matt Reeves, indicated that the script is likely nearing completion, though he did not confirm it was finished. This suggests a smooth transition into filming ahead of the movie's release date.

    Previously, in June 2024, there were concerns about the script's progress due to a nine-month hiatus without updates. DCU co-CEO James Gunn noted that the script's readiness depends on Reeves, emphasizing that quality is prioritized over a specific timeline. This approach aims to ensure the sequel meets the high standards of the original film.

    Back in September 2023, Warner Bros. Discovery prioritized completing the script following the end of the Writers Guild strikes. Although production delays were possible due to these strikes, the movie's release was postponed to 2026, allowing more time for a thorough production process, which is crucial for the film's detective elements.

THE BATMAN II RELEASE DATE

    The Batman experienced multiple delays due to the global pandemic, having begun filming just before lockdown and resuming production later in 2020. Initially set for a summer 2021 release, the sequel, The Batman - Part II, was later scheduled for October 3, 2025, but is now pushed to October 2, 2026, following a March 2024 update.

    This delay is attributed to disruptions caused by the SAG-AFTRA and WGA strikes, affecting several superhero projects. The extra time aims to ensure the sequel's quality, especially with upcoming franchise spinoffs in the interim. The new release date places The Batman - Part II after Avengers 5, potentially giving it a prime spot at the box office. However, the DCU's next Batman film, The Brave and the Bold, is also expected in 2026, meaning two Batman films may release in close succession. While this could complicate things, it may also signal a shift in DC's approach to competing with the MCU.

THE BATMAN II CAST: WHO COULD RETURN & WHO MIGHT DEBUT?

    So far, two cast members have been confirmed to return for The Batman - Part II: Robert Pattinson as Bruce Wayne and Colin Farrell as the Penguin. Farrell's return was announced during the 2024 San Diego Comic-Con, indicating that the events of the upcoming The Penguin series will influence the sequel. Given the tense relationship established between Batman and the Penguin in the first film, their dynamic will be intriguing, especially as the Penguin gains power.

    It's likely that Zoë Kravitz (Catwoman), Jeffrey Wright (Jim Gordon), and Andy Serkis (Alfred) will also return, along with Barry Keoghan, who may have a larger role as the Joker. Additionally, Paul Dano's Riddler, who survived the first film and has ties to the Joker, could become a significant threat in the trilogy.

THE BATMAN II STORY: HOW THE BATMAN'S ENDING SETS UP A SEQUEL

    The Batman concludes with a scene where The Riddler befriends the Joker in Arkham Asylum, suggesting a possible alliance for The Batman - Part II. The Joker's riddle hints at the emergence of the Court of Owls, a criminal organization comprising Gotham's elite. Pattinson’s Batman reflects in a voiceover that Gotham will attempt to rebuild, but chaos and corruption will peak following Carmine Falcone's death.

THE BATMAN II DOES NOT YET HAVE A TRAILER

    While The Batman - Part II is confirmed, a trailer won't be available soon since the sequel is still in the early stages of development, lacking both footage and a finalized story. Other DC films, like Joker: Folie à Deux, have released teaser visuals shortly after announcements, so it's possible The Batman - Part II could receive similar promotion in the future as more details emerge.

    With The Penguin set to release between the two films, it may make sense to debut a trailer for The Batman - Part II after the spinoff concludes, possibly aligning with its finale. Since the sequel is scheduled for 2026, there is ample time for a trailer release, allowing audiences to engage with the upcoming spinoff series in the meantime. Ultimately, the strategy may lean toward waiting to generate excitement closer to the film's actual release.

IS THE JOKER IN THE BATMAN II? LIKELY THE BATMAN - PART II VILLAINS EXPLAINED

    Given the Joker's appearance in The Batman, it's expected he will have a significant role in The Batman - Part II. A deleted scene shows Batman visiting the Joker in Arkham Asylum to inquire about the Riddler, indicating the Joker's involvement is likely to expand. Director Matt Reeves seems to be withholding details about the character, potentially reserving a major confrontation for the franchise's finale.

    Another possible antagonist for the sequel is the Court of Owls, which fits the film's themes of organized crime and corruption among Gotham's elite. With Gotham's situation worsening after the Riddler's actions, the Court could emerge as a central threat in The Batman - Part II. However, any details regarding the villain are still speculative, and the film is expected to provide a fresh take, much like Paul Dano's reinterpretation of the Riddler.

EVERYTHING WE KNOW ABOUT THE BATMAN SPIN-OFF SHOWS

    Warner Bros. has announced several spinoff shows set in The Batman universe. Initially, a series focused on the Gotham Police Department (Gotham P.D.) was planned for HBO Max but has been indefinitely put on hold due to internal disagreements. This was supposed to be replaced by an untitled Arkham Asylum series, which could have provided a proper introduction for the Joker. However, this series was also reportedly canceled in July 2024, although the possibility of a future project set in Arkham remains open.

    The most recent confirmed spinoff is a Penguin-focused show, set to debut in September 2024. This series will explore Oz Cobb's rise to power and the implications of Carmine Falcone's death on Gotham's criminal underworld, while connecting the timelines of the first and second films.

    While a Catwoman spinoff has not been confirmed, director Matt Reeves has expressed interest in developing one, especially given the success of The Batman and Zoë Kravitz's performance. Additionally, reports indicate Reeves is meeting with writers and directors to explore more series and movies featuring characters like Scarecrow, Clayface, and Professor Pyg. However, given the uncertainties within the DC Universe and Warner-Discovery, plans may change. Regardless, all these shows are expected to interconnect with The Batman - Part II.

WILL THE BATMAN II WILL BE PART OF THE NEW DC UNIVERSE?

    With Warner Bros. moving away from the Zack Snyder-created version of its DC heroes, some fans speculated that The Batman could launch a new universe. However, in January 2023, DC Studios co-head James Gunn confirmed that The Batman - Part II and related projects will remain separate from the broader DC Universe.

    These projects, including The Batman 2, will fall under the DC Elseworlds banner, which denotes alternate universes outside the main continuity. This decision aligns well with the darker, grittier tone of The Batman, distinguishing it from the more fantastical elements of the upcoming DC Universe releases.

    The new DC Universe will introduce its own version of Batman in Batman: The Brave and the Bold, which is expected to feature a lighter tone and the return of Robin to the big screen. This will create a parallel to Reeves' darker The Batman universe. It remains uncertain how audiences will react to having two different Batman series simultaneously, but for now, The Batman - Part II will have the creative freedom to explore its darker narrative without constraints.
